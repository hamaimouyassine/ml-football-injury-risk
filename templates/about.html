About this tool

What it does, how it works, and how to interpret the results.

What problem does it solve?

Professional players often return from injury without a clear idea of reinjury risk. This tool estimates the probability that a player will sustain another injury within 90 days after returning, using their profile, position, injury type, FIFA rating, and history. The goal is to support prevention and return-to-play decisions—not to replace medical expertise.

How to read the result

The model outputs a probability (0–100%) and a risk level:

  • Low (0–30%) — Standard monitoring and load management.
  • Medium (30–60%) — Gradual return and closer follow-up.
  • High (60–100%) — Careful rehab and medical oversight.

Dataset

European professional football injury data: one row per injury, multiple rows per player over time. ~500+ records after cleaning. Features include age, position, injury type, FIFA rating, absence duration, previous injury count, and days since last return.

Model performance

Classification model (Logistic Regression, Random Forest, or LightGBM). Best by ROC-AUC on a held-out test set is saved and used here.

Limitations

  • Based on historical data; may not generalise to all leagues or populations.
  • Does not replace clinical assessment or medical advice.
  • Inputs are simplified; real cases are more complex.
  • Performance depends on data quality and the 90-day window.
